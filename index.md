# COMP110 EX09: Office Hours and Help-Seeking

## Summary

For my EX09 project, I analyzed whether students who report needing office hours more often also report lower understanding and higher difficulty in COMP110. My goal was to see whether the course could create more value by improving how it encourages students to seek help earlier. I used the course survey data to compare office hours usage to student-reported understanding, difficulty, and course value.

## Research Question

Do students who report needing more help on assignments (more office hours visits) also report lower understanding and higher difficulty, and does this suggest COMP110 should improve how it supports students in seeking help earlier?

## Stakeholder

The primary stakeholder is students enrolled in COMP110. A secondary stakeholder is the instructional staff, since they can use this information to improve how the course encourages and structures help-seeking behavior.

## Conclusion

My analysis suggests that office hours usage is strongly connected to student learning experiences. If students who use office hours more often also report lower understanding and higher difficulty, this may mean many students seek help only after they are already struggling. Because of this, COMP110 may be able to create value by making help-seeking earlier, easier, and more normalized.
